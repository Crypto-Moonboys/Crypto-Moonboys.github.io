Moonboys How-To-Play & Systems Manual

This is the one-page player guide for the full ecosystem. If you ever asked “why did this popup happen?”, “why didn’t I get XP?”, or “what does Telegram sync actually do?” — this manual answers it in plain English.

🕹️ Arcade Score Layer Leaderboard Rank Layer 🏙️ Block Topia Survival Layer 🕵️ Covert Ops Layer 🔗 Telegram Identity Layer ⚡ XP + 💎 Gems Economy

A) What this website is

The Crypto Moonboys site is one connected ecosystem made of four public layers:

🕹️ Arcade

Free-to-play skill games where you generate score and compete.

📚 Wiki

The lore, factions, characters, events, and strategic context of the world.

⚔️ Battle Chamber

Community intelligence layer: missions, activity, and social coordination.

🏙️ Block Topia

Live RPG world where pressure, events, and survival mechanics run in real time.

They are connected, but not identical: score determines ranking, XP powers survival/progression, gems fuel upgrades, and your synced identity determines whether progress is persistent or browser-local.

B) Arcade explained

  • Arcade games are free to play.
  • Your run creates a score for the leaderboard.
  • Score is not XP. Leaderboard rank uses score, not your XP stash.
  • Accepted scores can convert into Block Topia XP when your Telegram identity is synced.
  • If you are unsynced, you can still play and post local run data, but long-term progression stays local/browser-only.

C) Telegram sync (required for stored progression)

Telegram sync is your identity handshake with the live system. Without it, the server cannot safely attach progression to a persistent player identity.

  1. Open the official bot: @WIKICOMSBOT.
  2. Run /gkstart (initial bot activation).
  3. Run /gklink.
  4. Open the link returned by the bot and complete link confirmation.
  5. Return to Arcade/Leaderboard/Block Topia and confirm sync is green/linked.
  • When linked: accepted score-to-XP conversion and progression can persist server-side.
  • If sync expires: your identity state can go red/invalid until you relink with /gklink.
  • If not linked: you can play, but progression is local-only and does not behave like full persistent progression.

D) Block Topia explained

Block Topia is the live world / RPG / node-control layer. It is built around pressure, survival, and response.

  • XP is required to enter and remain effective inside the world loop.
  • XP drains while active in Block Topia because survival has ongoing system cost.
  • Mini-games can cost XP to engage and may return XP/gems based on outcome.
  • World events are not cosmetic; they reflect current pressure across nodes/districts.
  • SAM pressure and district struggle are active forces in the loop, not background story text.

Controls

  • Move: W A S D or arrow keys.
  • Select node / player / tile: left-click.
  • Move to tile: double-click a valid tile.
  • Interact with NPC: E when in range.
  • Challenge player to duel: select them, then press F.
  • Zoom: mouse wheel or [ / ] preset steps.
  • Camera pan: left mouse button hold + drag.

D2) Covert Ops — Signal Runner

The Signal Runner is your covert operative. Deploy it to a node to shift that node's control state from the shadows. The server resolves every mission; the client shows the outcome.

How to deploy

  1. Click a control node on the map — the target ID appears in the Covert Ops panel.
  2. Press Deploy Signal Runner → [NODE] in the bottom-right panel.
  3. Wait ~30 seconds for the server to resolve the mission.
  4. Read the result in the feed and the panel: ✅ Success or ❌ Failure.

Mission odds and risks

  • Base success: 65%. SAM pressure and district posture reduce this.
  • Operative loss: on failure there is a 25% chance the operative is captured. A 🚨 Signal Runner lost message confirms it.
  • Only one operative can be active at a time. The deploy button is locked during an active mission.

Player heat

Every deploy and resolution adds heat to your personal counter (+8 on success, +16 on failure). Heat decays at 1 unit per second server-side. You cannot manually reduce it.

Heat tierRangeEffect
Cold0 – 24Normal. Best time to deploy.
Warm25 – 49SAM awareness rising. Consider waiting.
Hot50 – 74Detection risk elevated; viewport vignette activates.
Critical75 – 100Maximum risk. Operations heavily penalised.

Covert Grid strip (top of screen)

Four cells show live covert state: Network Heat (world-level risk across all players), Local Node (pressure on your selected node), District Mood (posture of your current district), and Agent Pressure (how many of your operatives are under SAM scrutiny).

Keep Network Heat and your personal heat low before committing to a mission for the best odds.

E) Why popups and events happen

Events appear because the world state demands a response (node pressure, district conflict, hostile system momentum, or instability windows). They are designed as state reactions, not random interruptions.

Outbreak spreads, survival cost can rise, district risk worsens. Skips immediate challenge but usually forfeits event rewards and does not provide full stabilisation value.
Firewall Defense Hostile traffic or SAM push is stressing a protected route. Defensive integrity drops and follow-up attacks are more likely. Route integrity restored; can preserve node control momentum. System leaks, penalty pressure increases, and chain events may trigger. Bypasses this defense check at a cost, but can reduce long-term upside.
Signal Routing The world needs active rerouting to keep sectors connected. Route quality degrades, causing weaker support to nearby objectives. Signal flow improves and supports progression efficiency. Misroutes generate instability and can produce extra event load. Can preserve momentum short-term, but you miss route-optimization rewards.
Circuit Breach / Reconnect Critical circuits were disrupted and need manual response. Disconnected systems stay vulnerable and drain opportunities. Reconnect restores functionality and often lowers pressure drift. Partial blackout continues, reducing safe progression windows. Skip prevents immediate failure, but no mastery reward and less resilience gain.
District War Pressure NPC and faction influence conflict reached active contest level. You give ground; district influence may swing away from your side. District control and morale improve; better conditions for later runs. Opposition momentum rises and district becomes harder to stabilise. Can avoid immediate losses, but cedes strategic value over time.

F) XP explained

  • XP is earned through accepted performance loops (arcade conversion, world actions, and event outcomes).
  • Accepted arcade scores are one major feed into XP.
  • Block Topia uses XP as active survival/progression currency.
  • Mini-games can consume XP to enter and can return XP if you perform well.
  • XP can go down because the world uses drain, failure pressure, and survival costs.
  • In practical terms: XP is not just “level points.” It is your run-time survival fuel.

G) Gems explained

  • Gems are earned from successful loops/events/mini-game outcomes.
  • Gems are not the gate to enter Block Topia.
  • Gems are used for upgrades and deeper RPG progression paths.
  • Upgrades improve later runs (efficiency, resilience, and pressure handling).

H) How to earn more (practical strategy)

Improve score quality

  • Master 2–3 games instead of spreading effort thinly.
  • Prioritise clean runs over risky early-score spikes.
  • Use leaderboard refresh cycles to target realistic rank jumps.

Convert score into XP

  • Stay Telegram-linked so accepted runs can persist/convert.
  • If sync turns red, relink before grinding long sessions.
  • Aim for consistency; accepted runs beat one-off spikes.

Survive longer in Block Topia

  • Enter with XP buffer, not minimum threshold mindset.
  • Respond to pressure events early to avoid compounding costs.
  • Use mini-games when expected reward outweighs current XP risk.

Earn and use gems better

  • Prioritise upgrades that improve repeat-run efficiency.
  • Do not burn resources on low-impact convenience too early.
  • Treat gems as long-cycle build currency, not panic spend currency.

Use skip carefully

  • Skip is for damage control, not default play style.
  • If you skip repeatedly, you often lose reward velocity and map influence.
  • Best use case: protect a high-value run state from cascade failure.

Exploit pressure moments

  • Pressure spikes often open high-impact response windows.
  • Winning during these moments improves both rewards and strategic position.
  • Think in loops: score → accepted run → XP → survival → better future runs.

I) World / NPC / District / SAM explainer

Block Topia is a living war-map, not a static menu. NPC groups recruit, reinforce, and fight for nodes. Districts shift under sustained pressure. SAM acts as the hostile systemic force: it drives instability, stress-tests control, and punishes passive play.

Your actions matter because every successful defense, route recovery, and pressure response influences how hard the next cycle hits. You are not just farming points — you are helping decide whether districts stabilise or collapse into hostile control.

J) FAQ / quick troubleshooting

I’m linked but can’t enter Block Topia

Most common causes: your current XP is too low for active survival, or your link state is stale. Recheck sync state and relink with /gklink if needed.

I played a game but got no XP

Leaderboard score and XP are separate. XP comes from accepted conversion/stateful systems, especially when identity is synced.

Why does it say local only?

Your session is currently unsynced. Runs still work, but progression is stored in your browser session, not full server-backed profile state.

What does “accepted score” mean?

An accepted score is a run the system validates for ranking/progression pathways. Only accepted runs are eligible for conversion effects.

Why did Block Topia kick me out?

Usually XP drain reached a critical floor, or pressure/failure chains exhausted your current survival state.

Why is sync red?

Your Telegram link likely expired or failed validation. Relink via /gklink and re-open the link from the bot.

What if auth expired?

Run /gklink again, complete the new link handshake, then refresh your game page before continuing long runs.

Signal Runner deploy button is greyed out

Either no node is selected (click a control node on the map first) or a mission is already running. Only one operative can be active at a time.

My Signal Runner keeps failing

Check your personal heat (shown as Heat: X in the Covert Ops panel). If heat is above 50, wait for it to decay below 25 before deploying again. Also check district mood — a pre_lockdown district heavily penalises success odds.
