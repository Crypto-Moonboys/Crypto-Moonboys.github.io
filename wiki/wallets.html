Crypto Wallets

✅ Verified📅 Last updated: 2024-01📂 Tools & Platforms
📋 Contents

Crypto wallets are software programs or physical hardware devices that store the cryptographic keys needed to access and transact cryptocurrency. Despite the name, wallets don't actually store crypto — the coins live on the blockchain. Wallets store the private keys that prove ownership and authorise transactions.

Hot Wallets vs Cold Wallets

Hot wallets are connected to the internet — convenient for frequent trading but exposed to online threats. Cold wallets (hardware wallets) store keys offline, providing maximum security for long-term holdings.

Seed Phrases

Most modern wallets use a seed phrase (mnemonic phrase) — typically 12 or 24 random words — as a master backup for all private keys. Anyone with your seed phrase has full access to your wallet. Never share your seed phrase with anyone and store it offline in a secure location.

🚨
Security Warning: Legitimate projects, support staff, and tools will NEVER ask for your seed phrase. Any request for your seed phrase is a scam.

Popular Wallets

  • MetaMask — Most popular Ethereum/EVM wallet browser extension
  • Phantom — Leading Solana wallet
  • Ledger Nano — Top hardware wallet for cold storage
  • Trust Wallet — Multi-chain mobile wallet (Binance-owned)
  • Rabby — Security-focused EVM wallet with transaction simulation

Not Your Keys, Not Your Coins

The crypto adage "Not your keys, not your coins" warns against leaving crypto on exchanges. The collapse of FTX in 2022 — where billions in user funds were lost — reinforced this principle. Self-custody via a personal wallet is recommended for significant holdings.
