Altcoins

✅ Verified📅 Last updated: 2024-01📂 Cryptocurrencies
📋 Contents

Altcoins is a portmanteau of alternative coins — referring to every cryptocurrency other than Bitcoin. The term encompasses thousands of digital assets ranging from major platforms like Ethereum and Solana to obscure community tokens and meme coins. Each altcoin typically aims to improve upon Bitcoin in some way or serve a specific use case.

Categories of Altcoins

  • Platform tokens — ETH, SOL, ADA, DOT — fuel decentralised platforms
  • Stablecoins — USDT, USDC, DAI — pegged to fiat currency
  • DeFi tokens — UNI, AAVE, CRV — governance and utility in DeFi
  • Meme coins — DOGE, SHIB, PEPE — community-driven speculative assets
  • Layer 2 tokens — ARB, OP, MATIC — Ethereum scaling solutions
  • Gaming/metaverse — AXS, SAND, MANA — in-game economies

Altcoin Season (Altseason)

Altcoin season (often called altseason) refers to periods when altcoins collectively outperform Bitcoin. Typically follows Bitcoin bull runs as capital rotates from BTC into higher-risk, higher-potential-reward assets. The Altcoin Season Index tracks whether alt markets are outperforming BTC.

💡
The crypto community joke: "First Bitcoin pumps, then ETH, then the altcoins, then your shitcoin, then it all crashes." — This loosely describes typical cycle behaviour.

Risks of Altcoins

  • Project failure — Most altcoins from past cycles are worth near zero today
  • Rug pulls — Anonymous developers abandon projects after raising funds
  • Low liquidity — Small caps can be easily manipulated
  • Regulatory risk — Many altcoins may be classified as securities

Community Views

Altcoins divide the crypto community. Bitcoin maximalists dismiss all altcoins. "Multi-coiners" diversify across ecosystems. The Moonboys community in particular is associated with following high-risk altcoin plays for maximum upside potential during bull markets.
