Crypto Exchanges

✅ Verified📅 Last updated: 2024-01📂 Tools & Platforms
📋 Contents

Crypto exchanges are platforms that enable users to buy, sell, and trade cryptocurrencies. They fall into two main categories: Centralised Exchanges (CEX) — operated by companies that hold user funds and require KYC — and Decentralised Exchanges (DEX) — smart contract-based platforms where users retain custody of their funds.

Centralised Exchanges (CEX)

CEXs are the most user-friendly entry point into crypto. They handle order matching, liquidity, and custody. Most require identity verification (KYC/AML). Leading CEXs include:

  • Binance — World's largest by volume
  • Coinbase — Most popular in the US, publicly listed
  • Kraken — Long-established, security-focused
  • OKX — Major global exchange
  • Bybit — Popular for derivatives trading
⚠️
FTX's 2022 collapse wiped out billions in user funds, demonstrating CEX counterparty risk. Consider withdrawing large holdings to self-custody wallets.

Decentralised Exchanges (DEX)

DEXs use smart contracts (typically AMMs) to enable trustless token swaps without a central operator. Users connect their wallet directly and retain custody throughout. Leading DEXs:

  • Uniswap — Largest Ethereum DEX, pioneer of AMM model
  • Jupiter — Leading Solana DEX aggregator
  • Curve Finance — Specialised in stablecoin trading
  • dYdX — Decentralised perpetuals trading
  • Pancakeswap — Leading BNB Chain DEX

Types of Trading

  • Spot trading — Direct exchange of one asset for another at current market price
  • Margin trading — Borrowed funds to amplify position size (high risk)
  • Futures/Perpetuals — Contracts to buy/sell at future price
  • Options — Right to buy/sell at specified price

Community Views

The "CEX vs DEX" debate is ongoing. DeFi maximalists advocate for self-custody DEX trading. Many retail traders prefer CEXs for their UX, fiat on-ramps, and customer support. Post-FTX, "not your keys, not your coins" has renewed interest in DEX usage.
