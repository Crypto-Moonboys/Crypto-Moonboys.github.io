Tokenomics

✅ Verified📅 Last updated: 2024-01📂 Concepts
📋 Contents

Tokenomics (a portmanteau of token economics) refers to the economic system governing a cryptocurrency or token. It encompasses all aspects of a token's supply, distribution, utility, and incentive mechanisms. Good tokenomics can be the difference between a project's long-term success and failure.

Supply Metrics

  • Max Supply — The hard cap on total tokens ever created (e.g., Bitcoin: 21M)
  • Circulating Supply — Tokens currently in circulation and tradeable
  • Total Supply — All minted tokens minus burned tokens
  • Fully Diluted Valuation (FDV) — Market cap if all tokens were in circulation

Token Distribution

How tokens are allocated matters enormously. Typical allocations include: team/founders (often 15-20%, usually vested), investors/VCs (10-20%), ecosystem/treasury fund (20-40%), public sale/IDO, and community rewards/liquidity mining. Cliff and vesting schedules — lockup periods before tokens can be sold — prevent immediate dumping by insiders.

⚠️
High insider allocation with short vesting schedules is a red flag. Always check a project's tokenomics before investing.

Token Utility

Token utility defines what the token is used for within its ecosystem. Strong utility creates organic demand:

  • Gas fees — ETH for Ethereum transactions
  • Governance — Vote on protocol changes (UNI, AAVE)
  • Staking — Earn rewards by locking tokens
  • Payments — Medium of exchange within an ecosystem
  • Burn mechanisms — Reducing supply to increase scarcity

Inflation vs Deflation

Inflationary tokens (like most PoS chains) issue new tokens as staking rewards, potentially diluting holders. Deflationary tokens reduce supply over time through burning — Ethereum post-EIP-1559 can be deflationary during high usage. Bitcoin is disinflationary — new supply halves every 4 years until ~2140.

Community Views

The crypto community has developed shorthand for evaluating tokenomics: "DYOR" (Do Your Own Research), "check the vesting schedule", and "who holds the bags" are common refrains. Twitter/X threads breaking down project tokenomics are a staple of crypto content creation.
