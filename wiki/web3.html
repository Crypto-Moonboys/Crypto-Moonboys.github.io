Web3

✅ Verified📅 Last updated: 2024-01📂 Technology
📋 Contents

Web3 is the vision of an internet owned by its users — not by Google, Facebook, Amazon, or any corporation. It's the internet where you own your data, your assets, your identity. Built on blockchain technology, powered by Ethereum and other networks, governed by communities through DAOs. Web3 is either the future of the internet or the biggest hype cycle ever. Probably both. But the infrastructure being built right now will matter regardless of what we call it.

History & Origins

Web 1.0 and Web 2.0 (1991–2014)

Web 1.0 (roughly 1991–2004) was the read-only internet: static HTML pages, GeoCities, AltaVista, and a web where you consumed content but never created it. Beautiful in its simplicity. Limited by design.

Web 2.0 (2004–present) was the read-write revolution: blogs, social media, user-generated content. YouTube, Facebook, Twitter, Instagram. The problem? Everything you created, contributed, and shared was owned by the platform. Your data was the product. Your attention was sold. The companies that built the "social web" extracted all the value from it.

Gavin Wood Coins "Web3" (2014)

In 2014, Gavin Wood — co-founder of Ethereum and later founder of Polkadot — coined the term "Web 3.0" in a blog post describing a future decentralized internet built on Ethereum as its foundational infrastructure layer. The vision: replace trusted intermediaries with cryptographic proof and smart contracts.

Ethereum launched in July 2015 as the practical foundation for that vision — a programmable blockchain enabling smart contracts, dApps, and eventually the entire DeFi and NFT ecosystem.

Building the Infrastructure (2015–2020)

IPFS (InterPlanetary File System) launched in 2015 by Juan Benet and Protocol Labs — a peer-to-peer hypermedia protocol for decentralized file storage. Where HTTP says "download this file from this server," IPFS says "find this file, wherever it lives." The decentralized storage backbone of Web3.

ENS (Ethereum Name Service) launched in 2017, enabling human-readable .eth domain names that map to Ethereum addresses. vitalik.eth instead of 0xd8dA6BF2.... Your Web3 identity.

MetaMask launched in 2016 as the browser extension that made Ethereum accessible — your wallet, your Web3 passport, your dApp browser. The Web3 on-ramp for tens of millions of users.

Killer Apps & The Hype Peak (2020–2022)

DeFi Summer 2020 was the first Web3 killer app moment — DeFi protocols exploded, TVL went from $1B to $15B in months, and the world suddenly understood that you could do finance on a blockchain without a bank. Then the NFT boom of 2021 brought NFTs into mainstream consciousness — digital ownership became a talking point for everyone from artists to athletes to Fortune 500 companies.

ConstitutionDAO (November 2021) raised $47 million in 5 days through a DAO to buy a copy of the US Constitution at Sotheby's. They lost the auction to a billionaire, but the world had seen DAOs in action. In the same year, Facebook rebranded to Meta, committing $10B+ to the metaverse — Decentraland and The Sandbox pumped accordingly.

The Ethereum Merge in September 2022 — transitioning from Proof-of-Work to Proof-of-Stake — was the most significant infrastructure upgrade in Web3 history, reducing Ethereum's energy consumption by ~99.95%.

Maturation & New Frontiers (2023–2024)

Web3 social emerged as a new frontier: Lens Protocol, Farcaster, and Friend.tech explored what social media looks like when users own their social graph and content. AI + Web3 convergence became a theme — decentralized AI compute, verifiable credentials, and identity projects like Worldcoin / World ID. Base (Coinbase's L2, launched 2023) brought a new wave of mainstream users into Web3 via Coinbase's existing user base.

Core Concepts

Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI)

In Web 2.0, your identity is a Facebook account or a Google login — controlled by a corporation that can delete you at will. In Web3, your identity is your wallet address and your .eth ENS name — cryptographically yours, controlled by no one but you. This is self-sovereign identity: you are the issuer, holder, and controller of your own credentials.

Decentralized Storage

  • IPFS — Content-addressed peer-to-peer storage; 280,000+ nodes globally
  • Arweave — Permanent, pay-once storage on a "permaweb"
  • Filecoin — Incentivized decentralized storage marketplace built on IPFS

ENS Domains

Your .eth name is your Web3 address — resolves to your Ethereum wallet, links to your avatar, social links, and on-chain identity. Over 2.5 million ENS names registered. Think of it as DNS for the decentralized internet.

DAOs — Decentralized Autonomous Organizations

DAOs replace traditional corporate governance with on-chain voting by token holders. No CEO, no board of directors — just smart contract rules and community governance. DAOs currently manage over $20 billion in treasuries. Examples: MakerDAO (governs DAI stablecoin), Uniswap DAO (governs the DEX), ConstitutionDAO (tried to buy the Constitution).

dApps & Smart Contracts

Decentralized applications run on smart contracts — code that lives on the blockchain and executes automatically without a central server. No single point of failure. No "we're down for maintenance." No ability to censor users. The backend of Web3.

The Ownership Economy

Web3's core thesis: the people who build, use, and contribute to a network should own a piece of it. Token-gating, governance tokens, creator monetization without platform cuts — the ownership economy is the antithesis of Web 2.0's "your engagement is our revenue" model. DeFi and NFTs are its two biggest expressions to date.

Why It Matters

Web3 matters because the alternative — a world where Google, Apple, Facebook, and Amazon control your digital identity, financial access, and online expression — has visible, documented problems. Censorship. Data exploitation. Platform deplatforming. De-banking.

Financial sovereignty starts with a wallet — your key to accessing a global, permissionless financial system. Censorship resistance is non-negotiable for billions of people in countries where free expression is dangerous. Creator monetization without a 30% platform tax changes the economics for artists, musicians, and writers. Cross-border transactions without SWIFT or Western Union fees open up financial access for the unbanked.

DeFi and NFTs are the current killer apps. The next generation of Web3 applications — decentralized social, AI-powered identity, programmable money — is being built right now. Composability ("money legos") means each new protocol can build on every existing one — a compounding innovation advantage that Web 2.0 walled gardens can never replicate.

Risks & Controversies

  • UX complexity: Seed phrases, gas fees, wallet addresses — Web3 onboarding is still a nightmare for normal people. Until this is solved, mass adoption will be limited.
  • The centralization paradox: Most NFT metadata is stored on AWS. Most Ethereum nodes run on Infura (which runs on AWS). The "decentralized" web often runs on centralized infrastructure. Moxie Marlinspike's 2022 essay on this remains the definitive critique.
  • VC capture: Early investors hold large token allocations and can dump on retail. The "decentralized" governance of many protocols is dominated by VC-funded foundations.
  • Regulatory uncertainty: SEC enforcement actions, EU MiCA regulations, and global crackdowns create legal risk for Web3 protocols, developers, and users.
  • Scams & phishing at scale: The permissionless nature of Web3 makes it an ideal environment for scammers. Phishing sites, fake mints, rug pulls, and social engineering are endemic.
  • Scalability limits: The Merge improved Ethereum's energy use but didn't solve gas fees or throughput. Layer 2 solutions (Arbitrum, Optimism, Base) help, but cross-L2 fragmentation creates new UX challenges.
  • Real adoption gap: Despite $33B in VC funding at peak, daily active Web3 users remain a fraction of Web 2.0 platforms. Most dApps have thousands of users, not millions.

The Crypto Moonboys Take 🌙

"We're not early. We're still on the launchpad. The rocket hasn't even ignited."

Web3 is the moon mission we're all on together. The infrastructure — Ethereum, Layer 2s, IPFS, ENS, DeFi, DAOs — is being built in real time by some of the smartest developers on the planet. The critics are right that we haven't reached mass adoption. They're wrong to think that means it won't happen.

WAGMI isn't just a meme — it's a prophecy. The internet of value, where you are the bank, the creator, the sovereign, and the owner — is being assembled block by block, transaction by transaction. Every wallet download, every ENS name registered, every DAO vote cast is a brick in that foundation. We are going to make it.
