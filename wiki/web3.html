Web3

✅ Verified📅 Last updated: 2024-01📂 Technology
📋 Contents

Web3 (also written Web 3.0) refers to the vision of a decentralised internet built on blockchain technology. It represents a shift away from the platform-dominated, data-harvesting Web 2.0 (Facebook, Google, Twitter) toward an internet where users own their data, digital assets, and identity — enabled by cryptography and blockchain.

Web Evolution

EraPeriodKey Characteristics
Web 1.01990s–2000sRead-only. Static pages. No interaction.
Web 2.02000s–presentRead-write. Social media. Centralised platforms own your data.
Web32020s+Read-write-own. Blockchain. Users control data and assets.

Key Pillars of Web3

  • Decentralisation — No single entity controls the network
  • Tokenisation — Digital ownership of assets and identity via NFTs and tokens
  • DAOs — Decentralised Autonomous Organisations replace traditional corporate governance
  • Self-custody — Users hold their own private keys and assets
  • Interoperability — Cross-chain protocols enable asset portability

Criticism & Debate

Web3 is highly contested. Supporters see it as the next evolution of the internet. Critics (including figures like Jack Dorsey) argue it is marketing hype, that VC firms control most token allocations, and that true decentralisation remains elusive. The debate between Web3 idealists and sceptics is one of crypto's most prominent ongoing discussions.

Community Views

The Web3 community spans DeFi enthusiasts, NFT collectors, DAO contributors, and metaverse builders. Twitter and Farcaster (a decentralised social protocol) are key gathering places. "Wen Web3" is a common sardonic phrase used within the community itself when projects fall short of decentralisation ideals.
