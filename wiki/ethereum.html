Ethereum (ETH)

✅ Verified 📅 Last updated: 2024-01 📂 Cryptocurrencies
📋 Contents

Ethereum (ETH) is the second-largest cryptocurrency by market capitalisation and the world's leading programmable blockchain platform. Founded by Vitalik Buterin and co-founders in 2013–2015, Ethereum introduced the concept of smart contracts — self-executing code stored on the blockchain — enabling a vast ecosystem of decentralised applications (dApps), DeFi protocols, NFTs, and more.

History

Conception & ICO (2013–2015)

In late 2013, then-19-year-old Vitalik Buterin published the Ethereum whitepaper, proposing a generalised blockchain that could run arbitrary programs. After a successful crowdsale (ICO) in 2014 that raised approximately $18 million in Bitcoin, the Ethereum mainnet launched on 30 July 2015.

Buterin's core insight was that Bitcoin's scripting language was too limited. Ethereum introduced a Turing-complete virtual machine (the EVM — Ethereum Virtual Machine) allowing developers to write arbitrary logic on the blockchain.

The DAO Hack & Fork (2016)

In 2016, a vulnerability in "The DAO" — a decentralised venture fund built on Ethereum — was exploited, draining ~$60 million in ETH. The Ethereum community controversially voted to hard fork the blockchain to reverse the theft. The minority that refused the fork continued as Ethereum Classic (ETC). This event remains a defining moment in blockchain governance history.

The Merge (2022)

On 15 September 2022, Ethereum completed "The Merge" — transitioning from energy-intensive Proof of Work (PoW) to Proof of Stake (PoS). This reduced Ethereum's energy consumption by approximately 99.95% and was a landmark technical achievement years in the making.

How It Works

Smart Contracts

Smart contracts are self-executing programs deployed on the Ethereum blockchain. They run exactly as programmed without downtime, censorship, fraud, or third-party interference. Common use cases include token issuance (ERC-20), NFT standards (ERC-721), DeFi protocols, and DAOs.

Gas Fees

Every transaction on Ethereum requires gas — a fee paid in ETH to compensate validators for processing the transaction. Gas fees fluctuate based on network demand. The EIP-1559 upgrade (August 2021) introduced a base fee burn mechanism, making ETH deflationary during periods of high usage.

Proof of Stake

Since The Merge, Ethereum uses PoS consensus. Validators stake a minimum of 32 ETH to participate in block production and earn staking rewards. This replaced miners with validators, dramatically reducing energy use and enabling further scaling upgrades.

Ecosystem

Ethereum hosts the largest ecosystem in crypto:

  • DeFi — Uniswap, Aave, Compound, MakerDAO, and hundreds more
  • NFTs — OpenSea, Blur, major NFT collections (BAYC, CryptoPunks)
  • Stablecoins — USDC, USDT, DAI issued as ERC-20 tokens
  • Layer 2s — Arbitrum, Optimism, Polygon, zkSync scaling Ethereum
  • DAOs — Decentralised autonomous organisations

Community Views

Ethereum's community is one of the most active in crypto. Proponents often describe ETH as "ultra sound money" due to its deflationary mechanics post-EIP-1559 and post-Merge. Critics, particularly Bitcoin maximalists, argue Ethereum is too complex and centralised.

"Ethereum is not just money — it's a platform for the entire digital economy." — Common sentiment in the Ethereum community[1]

Crypto YouTubers and bloggers have extensively covered Ethereum's "ultrasound money" narrative, pointing to ETH burn mechanics as a key bullish driver.[2]
