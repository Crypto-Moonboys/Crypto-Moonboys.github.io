DeFi (Decentralised Finance)

✅ Verified📅 Last updated: 2024-01📂 Concepts
📋 Contents

Decentralised Finance (DeFi) refers to a broad ecosystem of financial applications and protocols built on public blockchains — primarily Ethereum — that recreate and extend traditional financial services (lending, borrowing, trading, insurance) without centralised intermediaries like banks or brokerages.

Overview

DeFi protocols use smart contracts to automate financial logic. Anyone with a crypto wallet and an internet connection can access DeFi services globally, 24/7, without identity verification or approval from a central authority. This "permissionless" access is a key value proposition for the billions of people underserved by traditional finance.

Key Concepts

Automated Market Makers (AMMs)

Traditional exchanges use order books; DeFi DEXs use AMMs — algorithmic pricing models using liquidity pools. Users deposit token pairs into pools and earn fees from trades. Uniswap pioneered the constant-product formula (x * y = k) that most AMMs use.

Yield Farming & Liquidity Mining

Users earn returns by providing liquidity or capital to DeFi protocols, often receiving the protocol's governance token as additional incentive. Returns can be extremely high (and extremely risky), leading to the "degen" culture in DeFi communities.

Lending & Borrowing

Protocols like Aave and Compound allow users to deposit collateral and borrow other assets, or earn yield on deposits. Interest rates adjust algorithmically based on supply and demand.

Decentralised Stablecoins

MakerDAO's DAI is a crypto-collateralised stablecoin soft-pegged to the USD. Users lock ETH or other assets to mint DAI. This system enables DeFi access without exposure to fiat rails.

Risks

⚠️
High Risk: DeFi carries smart contract risk, oracle manipulation risk, impermanent loss, rug pulls, and extreme market volatility. Only invest what you can afford to lose.
  • Smart contract bugs — Code exploits have led to hundreds of millions in losses
  • Impermanent loss — Liquidity providers can lose compared to holding
  • Rug pulls — Anonymous teams can drain project funds
  • Oracle manipulation — Price feed attacks can destabilise protocols

Community Views

DeFi has its own passionate sub-culture. "Degens" — short for degenerates — is a self-applied label for risk-tolerant DeFi participants who chase high-yield opportunities. Crypto Twitter, Reddit's r/defi, and Discord servers are primary hubs for DeFi discussion and alpha-sharing.

"DeFi is going to eat traditional finance. It's just a matter of time." — Recurring sentiment on Crypto Twitter[1]
