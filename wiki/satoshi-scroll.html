The Satoshi Scroll

🌙 Community Lore 📅 Last updated: March 2026 📂 Lore
📋 Contents

The Satoshi Scroll is the mythologised version of the Bitcoin Whitepaper[1] in the HODL Wars universe. It is said to contain the laws of the financial revolution: decentralisation, self-custody, and HODLing. All HODL Warriors study The Scroll before their first Moon Mission.

The Real Whitepaper

The Bitcoin Whitepaper[1] — formally titled "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System" — was published on 31 October 2008 by Satoshi Nakamoto. At just nine pages, it described a system for electronic transactions that required no trust in a central authority, solving the long-standing double-spend problem through cryptographic proof and a distributed timestamp server.

The document is among the most consequential technical papers of the 21st century, spawning an entire industry worth trillions of dollars and a philosophical movement centred on financial sovereignty. In the HODL Wars lore, its nine pages are treated as the equivalent of a sacred scripture — studied, memorised, and quoted by warriors across the ecosystem.

Sacred Laws of the Scroll

The Scroll distils into three sacred laws that govern all HODL Warriors:

Law I — Decentralisation: No single entity controls the network. Power is distributed. This is not merely a technical property; in the lore, it is a philosophical stance against all forms of financial centralisation and censorship.

Law II — Self-Custody: Your keys, your coins. Every HODL Warrior must hold their own private keys, maintaining true ownership of their assets without dependence on any third party. The Scroll's most repeated verse:

"Not your keys, not your coins."[3]

Law III — HODLing: Patience is the supreme virtue. The Scroll teaches that markets are noisy in the short term but directional in the long term, and that the warrior who holds through every storm will ultimately reach the Moon Mission.

The Lost Satoshi

The mythology of the Satoshi Scroll is deepened by the mystery of its author[2]. Satoshi Nakamoto — the name behind Bitcoin's creation — has never been definitively identified. They disappeared from public communication in 2010, leaving behind an estimated 1 million unmoved BTC (the "Satoshi coins") and an identity that has become the central mystery of the crypto age.

In the HODL Wars lore, Satoshi is not merely a person but a mythological figure — the ultimate Diamond Hands warrior, the author of the sacred text, the architect of decentralisation itself. Their identity remains sealed, their coins unspent, their motives pure. They are the patron saint of every HODL Warrior who has ever refused to sell.
