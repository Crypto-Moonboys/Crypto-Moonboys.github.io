The HODL Warriors

🌙 Community Lore 📅 Last updated: March 2026 📂 Lore
📋 Contents

The HODL Warriors are the elite fighters of the HODL Wars universe — the true believers who never sell, never waver, and never surrender. Armed with Diamond Hands and guided by the sacred wisdom of The Satoshi Scroll, they represent the highest ideal of the crypto faithful.

Origins

The HODL Warriors emerged from the original HODL movement[1], born on the BitcoinTalk forums in December 2013. During a severe market correction, a passionate Bitcoin holder — in a state of spirited defiance — posted a now-legendary declaration: "I AM HODLING." The misspelling was intentional in spirit if not in letter, and the cry spread like wildfire across the nascent crypto community.

They are the elite fighters of the crypto lore universe who never sell. They HODL through every dip, every bear market siege, every rug pull. Armed with Diamond Hands and guided by The Satoshi Scroll, they march toward the Moon Mission.

Over time, the HODL Warrior archetype absorbed influence from the broader meme-stock movements of 2021[3], particularly the diamond hands emoji combination 💎🙌 popularised on r/WallStreetBets, which gave warriors a new visual identity and battle standard.

Code of the Warrior

Every HODL Warrior swears by the sacred code — a set of principles handed down through the lore, inscribed in the margins of The Satoshi Scroll, and repeated as mantras in every bull run and bear siege:

  • Never sell at a loss. The dip is temporary. The moon is eternal.
  • DYOR — Do Your Own Research. No influencer, no shiller, no anonymous tip supersedes personal due diligence.
  • Not your keys, not your coins. Self-custody is sovereignty. A warrior who relies on an exchange is a warrior who can be disarmed.
  • WAGMI — We're All Gonna Make It. The rallying cry of solidarity. Victory belongs to those who hold the line together.
  • Stack sats. Accumulate relentlessly during the siege. The bear market is the warrior's greatest recruiting ground.

Famous Warriors

The chronicles are filled with legendary warrior archetypes — figures who exemplify the HODL Warrior spirit and whose stories are told around virtual campfires:

The Genesis HODLer — The original BitcoinTalk poster whose defiant declaration launched the movement. Their identity is unknown, their Bitcoin address untouched for over a decade.

The Pizza Day Witness — A warrior who witnessed Laszlo Hanyecz pay 10,000 BTC for two pizzas in 2010 and, crucially, did not sell their own holdings at that price. In the lore, they are the patron saint of patience.

The Bear Market Survivor — The archetypal warrior who held through the 2018 crash from $20,000 to $3,200, then through the 2022 crash from $69,000 to $16,000, and emerged to see new all-time highs. Their armour bears the scars of every siege.

Enemies

The HODL Warriors face adversaries from multiple fronts. Their chief antagonists are the Paper Hands capitulators — not truly enemies, but fallen warriors whose weakness drags down market sentiment and creates the very dips that test Diamond Hands resolve.

More dangerous still are the shadow forces documented in the NGMI Chronicles — the scammers, the rug-pulling developers, the FUD-spreaders, and the influencers who shill projects for personal gain. These are the true villains of the HODL Wars, whose actions have cost the community fortunes and shaken faith.
