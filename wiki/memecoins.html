Meme Coins

✅ Verified📅 Last updated: 2024-01📂 Cryptocurrencies
📋 Contents

Memecoins started as a joke. Dogecoin launched on a meme. And then it hit a $90 billion market cap. Then came Shiba Inu. Then Pepe. Then BONK. Then WIF. Then TRUMP. Memecoins are the purest expression of the crypto casino — zero utility, maximum community, vibes-backed financial instruments that can turn $100 into $100,000 or into $0 with equal probability. Welcome to the most chaotic, most entertaining, and most dangerous corner of crypto.

History & Origins

The OG: Dogecoin (2013)

On December 6, 2013, software engineers Billy Markus and Jackson Palmer launched Dogecoin as a joke based on the viral Shiba Inu "Doge" meme from Reddit. They did not expect it to survive. It survived. The community became famous for tipping content creators, funding the Jamaican bobsled team's 2014 Winter Olympics trip, and sponsoring a NASCAR driver. DOGE spent years as a lovable meme with a ~$300M market cap — until Elon Musk got on Twitter.

On May 8, 2021, Elon Musk appeared on Saturday Night Live. The build-up and broadcast pushed DOGE to its all-time high of $0.7376, a market cap of ~$90 billion — larger than most Fortune 500 companies. People who bought in 2013 became multi-millionaires overnight.

Shiba Inu — "The DOGE Killer" (2020)

In August 2020, an anonymous developer known only as "Ryoshi" launched Shiba Inu (SHIB), explicitly marketing it as the "DOGE killer." 50% of the total supply was sent to Ethereum founder Vitalik Buterin — who proceeded to donate approximately $1 billion worth of SHIB to COVID-19 relief in India and burn ~$6.7 billion more. SHIB reached its all-time high of $0.00008845 on October 27, 2021, meaning someone who bought $8 of SHIB at launch had $5.7 billion at the peak. That is not a typo.

The Second Wave: PEPE, BONK, WIF (2022–2024)

BONK launched as a Christmas airdrop on December 25, 2022 to Solana NFT holders and degens — a community-driven resurrection of the Solana ecosystem after the FTX collapse nearly killed it. BONK became a symbol of Solana's survival. In April 2023, Pepe (PEPE) — based on Matt Furie's Pepe the Frog — reached a $1.6 billion market cap within weeks of launch, proving the memecoin supercycle was not over. Dogwifhat (WIF) launched in November 2023 and peaked at $4.89 with a market cap above $4.5 billion in January 2025.

The endless memecoin machine continued with FLOKI, BRETT, POPCAT, and MOODENG (based on a viral baby hippo video). pump.fun on Solana launched a factory for memecoins, creating over 1 million tokens and generating tens of millions in protocol revenue.

Political Memecoins (2025)

On January 17, 2025 — days before his inauguration — Donald Trump personally launched the TRUMP memecoin. It hit $75 within hours then crashed over 90%. On January 19, 2025, Melania Trump launched the MELANIA memecoin, just 3 days before the inauguration. Political memecoins had officially entered the mainstream.

How They Work

Memecoins are technically simple. Most are standard ERC-20 (Ethereum) or SPL (Solana) tokens with no special smart contract logic. On Solana, pump.fun provides a one-click launch pad where anyone can create a memecoin in seconds with a bonding curve mechanism. Value is entirely community-driven — there are no dividends, no revenue share, no governance rights, no utility. Price is determined purely by supply, demand, and vibes.

The typical memecoin lifecycle:

  1. Token created, small initial community seeded via Telegram/Twitter/Discord
  2. Influencer or KOL tweets about it — first pump
  3. CEX listing rumour or actual listing — second pump, massive volume
  4. Early buyers and team sell into the pump — price drops
  5. Repeat 2–3× if community is strong enough, then slow bleed to near-zero

Why People Buy Memecoins

Let's be honest. People buy memecoins for one reason: the 1000x dream. Unlike altcoins with complex tokenomics and VC unlocks suppressing price, memecoins often have simple supply structures and no VC overhang — meaning retail can actually win. Beyond pure speculation, memecoins serve as an entry point to crypto for people who understand internet culture better than financial jargon. They build genuine communities. They're tradeable on any exchange or DEX. And occasionally, they create life-changing wealth for people who would never have touched traditional finance.

  • Community building: DOGE funded real charitable causes. BONK saved a blockchain ecosystem
  • Entry point to crypto: Many people's first crypto experience is buying DOGE on Coinbase
  • Cultural relevance: Memecoins are crypto's pop culture. They're unavoidable
  • Asymmetric upside: A $100 bet on SHIB in 2020 was worth $5.7 billion at ATH

Risks & Controversies

💀
99% of memecoins go to zero. The ones that don't are statistical anomalies, not the norm.
  • Rug pulls: See Rug Pull Wars — developer drains liquidity after launch
  • FOMO buying at tops: See FOMO Plague — most retail buys at the peak
  • Team dumps: Creator wallets selling into community pumps
  • Honeypots: Tokens where you can buy but the smart contract prevents selling
  • Tax tokens: Hidden buy/sell tax encoded in the contract that steals percentage of every trade
  • SEC pressure: Regulatory scrutiny increasing, especially on politically connected tokens
  • Low liquidity: $50K sells can move price 30% on smaller memecoins

The NGMI Chronicles is a thorough archive of memecoin disasters. Study it before you buy anything trending on Twitter.

The Crypto Moonboys Take

"We're all just here for a good time. But the people who held DOGE from 2013 are certified GIGACHADs."

Diamond hands on memecoins is a special kind of insanity — but the DOGE OGs who never sold proved it can work. The BONK Christmas miracle of 2022 is Moonboys lore: a free airdrop to Solana NFT holders turned into billions in market cap and revived an entire ecosystem. That's the memecoin magic formula when it works.

The ugly truth? Most memecoin tokenomics are terrible. Infinite supply, no burns, whale wallets holding 20%+ of supply, no buy pressure mechanics. The ones that survive long-term are the exceptions. Play the pump. Know your exit. Don't get married to a dog coin.
