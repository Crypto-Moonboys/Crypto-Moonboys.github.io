NGMI Chronicles

🌙 Community Lore 📅 Last updated: March 2026 📂 Lore
📋 Contents

The NGMI Chronicles are the collected records of those who fell in the HODL Wars — sold at the bottom, fell for Rug Pulls, caught the FOMO Plague, or simply had Paper Hands. The Chronicles serve as cautionary tales for new warriors. Contrast with The WAGMI Prophecy.

Notable NGMI Cases

The Chronicles contain thousands of entries, but certain archetypes recur with such frequency that they have become legendary case studies in the HODL Wars curriculum:

The $1 Bitcoin Seller: Early Bitcoin holders who sold their coins at $1, $10, or $100 are the founding entries of the NGMI Chronicles. Their decisions — rational by any standard analysis available at the time — resulted in some of history's most costly exits. These entries are treated with sympathy, not mockery; they serve as the reminder that conviction in an early-stage revolution is extraordinarily difficult.

The $10 Ethereum Exit: Ethereum's early crowdsale participants who sold before the DeFi summer or the 2021 bull run missed returns of thousands of percent. Many had sound reasons for selling — profit-taking, tax obligations, genuine doubt about the technology's viability. The Chronicles record their cases as evidence that even well-reasoned exits can be tragic in hindsight.

The ATH Buyer: The inverse of the early seller. Warriors who entered the market at all-time highs, driven by FOMO, and then held through devastating drawdowns, or worse, sold at the bottom in despair. These entries are the most instructive, as they demonstrate the compounding damage of FOMO + Paper Hands acting in sequence.

The NGMI Hall of Shame

The Hall of Shame is the Chronicles' most-visited section — a curated list of the most iconic NGMI moments in crypto history[1]:

  • Selling 10,000 BTC for two pizzas (2010) — The Pizza Day transaction, honoured for its historical significance but mourned for its price.
  • Selling Bitcoin at the MtGox peak (~$32) in 2011 before the subsequent 93% crash — only to watch Bitcoin eventually reach $69,000.
  • Panic selling during the COVID crash of March 2020 — those who sold at $4,000 watched Bitcoin trade above $60,000 twelve months later.
  • The serial ICO investors of 2017 who funded hundreds of scam projects instead of simply holding Bitcoin.

Redemption: From NGMI to WAGMI

The Chronicles are not without hope. A significant portion of the collection is dedicated to redemption arcs — cases where warriors who appeared in NGMI entries found their way to WAGMI.

The path from NGMI to WAGMI runs through the same stages every time: the humbling loss, the education, the patient accumulation, and the test of conviction. Warriors who survived their NGMI moments and chose to learn from them rather than abandon the space entirely often emerged as the most reliable Diamond Hands in the community — their scar tissue having been transformed into armour.

The Chronicles hold that no warrior is permanently NGMI. Every loss is a lesson. Every rug pull survived is a degree from the school of DYOR. The WAGMI Prophecy promises that all who persist and learn will ultimately make it — and the Chronicles exist to accelerate that learning, not to condemn those who are still on the path. Transformation from NGMI to WAGMI begins with study of these very pages, and by building toward Diamond Hands.
