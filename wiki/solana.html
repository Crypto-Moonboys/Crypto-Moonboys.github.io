Solana (SOL)

✅ Verified📅 Last updated: 2024-01📂 Cryptocurrencies
📋 Contents

Solana (SOL) showed up to the blockchain party and said: "Speed? We do speed." While Ethereum was charging $50 gas fees during DeFi Summer, Solana was processing transactions in 400 milliseconds for fractions of a cent. Dismissed by critics as centralised, knocked down by network outages, nearly killed by the FTX collapse — Solana refused to die. Instead, it came back harder, faster, and louder, becoming THE home of memecoins, high-frequency DeFi, and the next wave of consumer crypto. The comeback kid of crypto. The chain that BONK built. SOL szn is eternal.

History & Origins

The Whitepaper & Founding (2017–2020)

Anatoly Yakovenko, a former Qualcomm engineer, published the Solana whitepaper in November 2017, introducing a novel concept called Proof of History (PoH) — a cryptographic clock that timestamps events on the blockchain before consensus, dramatically accelerating the ability to reach agreement without communication overhead. Yakovenko teamed up with Greg Fitzgerald and Eric Williams, eventually co-founding Solana Labs.

The Solana mainnet beta launched in March 2020, just as the world was locking down for COVID-19. Timing-wise, it was perfect: a new, fast blockchain ready just as DeFi was about to explode.

The FTX/Alameda Era & First ATH (2021)

The rocket fuel for Solana's first bull run came from an unexpected source: Sam Bankman-Fried and his firms FTX and Alameda Research became massive early investors and backers of the Solana ecosystem. FTX exchange promoted SOL heavily, funded Solana-based projects through its ventures arm, and was instrumental in bootstrapping the ecosystem.

The results were spectacular: SOL launched at under $1, rallied through 2021, and hit an all-time high of $260 on 6 November 2021. The Solana ecosystem saw explosive growth in NFTs (Degenerate Ape Academy, Okay Bears), DeFi (Serum DEX, Raydium), and gaming. Exchanges listed SOL widely as the "Ethereum killer" narrative peaked.

The FTX Collapse & Near-Death Experience (2022)

Then came November 2022. The FTX collapse — the most catastrophic fraud in crypto history — hit Solana harder than almost any other chain. When FTX went bankrupt, it became clear that Alameda Research held massive SOL positions, and the broader market panicked about what unwinding would mean for the ecosystem.

SOL crashed from ~$36 to a low of ~$8 — a 97% drawdown from its ATH. Developers left. Projects pivoted to other chains. Crypto Twitter wrote Solana's obituary weekly. The network's history of outages (over 17 major incidents between 2020–2022) added to the doom narrative. It looked like the end.

It wasn't.

BONK & the Community Revival (December 2022)

On 25 December 2022 — Christmas Day — the Solana community received an unlikely gift: the BONK airdrop. BONK, a Shiba Inu-inspired memecoin with a dog wielding a bat, airdropped 50% of its supply to Solana community members — NFT holders, developers, users. It was a community-driven move specifically designed to counter the Alameda/FTX token dominance and reinvigorate morale.

It worked beyond anyone's expectations. BONK rallied thousands of percent, brought in new users, dominated crypto Twitter, and reignited belief in Solana. The BONK airdrop is now legendary as one of the most effective community-building events in crypto history. Solana's comeback had begun. See Meme Coins for the full memecoin chapter.

The Great Comeback (2023–2024)

2023 was Solana's proof-of-resilience year. While Ethereum dealt with L2 fragmentation and the broader market remained bearish, Solana quietly rebuilt:

  • Magic Eden became the dominant NFT marketplace, expanding multi-chain but keeping Solana as its home base
  • Jupiter Exchange (jup.ag) emerged as the dominant DEX aggregator on Solana, processing billions in volume and becoming a top-10 DeFi protocol by usage
  • Memecoin mania arrived in force: BONK, dogwifhat (WIF), POPCAT, BRETT, and dozens of others launched on Solana, attracting a new generation of degens. The Pump.fun launchpad made creating memecoins accessible to anyone with $2 and a wallet
  • Network stability improved dramatically — the engineering team addressed the root causes of previous outages with QUIC networking, stake-weighted QoS, and fee market improvements

SOL set a new all-time high of $293 in January 2025, surpassing its previous 2021 peak. The "it's dead" crowd had been decisively proven wrong.

Solana ETF Filings (2025)

Following the Bitcoin and Ethereum ETF approvals, asset managers including VanEck and 21Shares filed for spot Solana ETFs in the US in 2025. While approval remains pending as of mid-2025, the filings signal growing institutional interest in SOL as an investment vehicle. Solana's DeFi ecosystem has grown to over $10B+ TVL by 2025.

How It Works

Proof of History (PoH)

Proof of History is Solana's core innovation — and the hardest thing to explain at a dinner party. Think of it as a cryptographic clock. Instead of nodes having to communicate with each other to agree on when something happened (which is slow), Solana creates a verifiable, trustless sequence of events using a Verifiable Delay Function (VDF). Specifically, it uses SHA-256 as a sequential hash function — each hash output becomes the input of the next, creating an unforgeable historical record of the passage of time and order of events. This means validators can process transactions without waiting for network-wide agreement on timestamps, massively accelerating throughput.

Tower BFT Consensus

Tower BFT is Solana's implementation of Practical Byzantine Fault Tolerance (PBFT), optimised to take advantage of the PoH clock. Because nodes share a common timestamp from PoH, the communication overhead of traditional BFT consensus is dramatically reduced. Validators vote on blocks, and votes are weighted by stake. Achieving finality (irreversible confirmation) takes approximately 32 validator votes — which, given Solana's block times, means economic finality in ~1–2 seconds.

Turbine & Gulf Stream

Turbine is Solana's block propagation protocol, inspired by BitTorrent. Instead of broadcasting full blocks to every node (which becomes a bottleneck at scale), Turbine breaks blocks into small packets and distributes them through a tree structure, dramatically reducing bandwidth requirements per validator.

Gulf Stream is Solana's mempool-less transaction forwarding protocol. Instead of transactions waiting in a mempool to be picked up, validators forward transactions to the next expected leaders in the validator schedule. This means the network can handle up to 100,000 transactions in memory before they even get processed, eliminating mempool congestion.

Sealevel: Parallel Execution

Sealevel is Solana's smart contract runtime, and it's what allows Solana to execute thousands of smart contracts in parallel using all available CPU and GPU cores. Unlike Ethereum's EVM, which processes transactions sequentially, Sealevel analyses which transactions access overlapping state and runs non-overlapping transactions simultaneously. This is the key architectural reason Solana can sustain much higher throughput than sequential execution chains.

Performance Numbers

The combined effect of PoH + Tower BFT + Turbine + Gulf Stream + Sealevel is a blockchain that delivers:

  • 400ms block times (versus Bitcoin's 10 minutes, Ethereum's ~12 seconds)
  • 65,000+ TPS theoretical maximum (real-world sustained: 3,000–5,000 TPS under normal conditions)
  • Transaction fees averaging <$0.001 — essentially free for end users
  • ~1–2 second economic finality

Why It Matters

Solana is proving that high-performance blockchain and decentralisation don't have to be mutually exclusive (with ongoing work to improve validator decentralisation). Here's why it matters:

  • Consumer Crypto: Solana is where mainstream consumer applications are being built. Helium (decentralised wireless), Hivemapper (decentralised mapping), and Render Network (GPU computing) all migrated to or launched on Solana for its speed and cost efficiency.
  • Payments: Visa and Shopify have both integrated Solana-based payment solutions, processing real-world commerce on the blockchain. USDC on Solana is a leading payment stablecoin.
  • DeFi: Jupiter Exchange (DeFi) processes billions in daily volume. Drift Protocol, MarginFi, Kamino Finance, and others form a complete DeFi ecosystem. The speed and cost of Solana make sophisticated on-chain strategies viable for retail users.
  • NFTs: NFTs on Solana via Magic Eden offer near-instant settlement and fees measured in cents, not dollars. The Solana NFT ecosystem has processed billions in lifetime volume.
  • Memecoins: Let's be honest — Solana is the memecoin capital of the world. Pump.fun has launched tens of thousands of tokens. BONK, WIF, POPCAT — these are Solana-native cultural phenomena. See Meme Coins.

Risks & Controversies

  • Network Outages: Solana suffered over 17 major network outages or slowdowns between 2020 and 2022, often lasting hours. The root causes ranged from bot spam during NFT mints to consensus bugs. Significant engineering effort has been put into stability improvements, and 2023–2024 have been much more stable — but the reputation damage lingers, and the risk isn't fully eliminated.
  • VC Token Distribution: Early venture capital investors received large SOL allocations at prices far below public sale. This creates overhang risk — large holders who can sell at significant profit. Critics argue Solana's token distribution is less decentralised than Bitcoin or Ethereum.
  • The FTX Stigma: The FTX connection nearly killed Solana. While the ecosystem has demonstrably moved past it, the association with Sam Bankman-Fried remains a reputational mark that FUD-spreaders continue to deploy.
  • Validator Concentration: While Solana has 1,500+ validators, stake is somewhat concentrated among large operators. The Nakamoto Coefficient (minimum validators needed to collude for 33% of stake) is lower than Ethereum, though improving.
  • Solana vs. Ethereum: The competition is real. Ethereum's L2 ecosystem has dramatically closed the fee gap post-Dencun. Whether Solana maintains its performance advantages as Ethereum scales is an open question.
