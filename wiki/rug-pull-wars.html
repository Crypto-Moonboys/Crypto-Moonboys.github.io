Rug Pull Wars

🌙 Community Lore 📅 Last updated: March 2026 📂 Lore
📋 Contents

The Rug Pull Wars are the treacherous chapter of the HODL Wars where unscrupulous developers abandon projects after collecting investor funds. Often preceded by the FOMO Plague, rug pulls are documented in the NGMI Chronicles as great betrayals — acts of war carried out not on the battlefield but in the darkness of anonymous wallets and deleted Telegram channels.

What is a Rug Pull

A rug pull is a type of crypto exit scam in which developers or project founders[1] abandon a project and abscond with investor funds — typically after artificially inflating the token's value through hype, marketing, and influencer promotion. The term references the idiom of pulling a rug out from under someone, leaving them suddenly on the floor with nothing.

Rug pulls typically follow a predictable pattern:

  1. Launch Phase: Anonymous developers create a token, NFT project, or DeFi protocol. They build community on Telegram and Discord, promising revolutionary technology.
  2. Hype Phase: Influencers are paid to shill. The FOMO Plague spreads. Retail investors pour funds in, driving the price up.
  3. Pull Phase: Developers drain the liquidity pool, dump their allocated tokens, and disappear. Social channels go silent. The token price collapses to near zero.
  4. Aftermath: Victims are left holding worthless tokens. The rug pull enters the NGMI Chronicles.

Famous Rug Pulls in Crypto History

The chronicles record numerous infamous rug pulls that have become cautionary legends:

Squid Game Token (2021)[2] — Capitalising on the viral Netflix show, this BSC token surged over 75,000% before developers drained approximately $3.38 million in liquidity and vanished. Holders were left unable to sell due to a contract that blocked all selling — a hard rug of maximum cynicism.

Frosties NFT (2022)[3] — The developers of the Frosties NFT collection rug-pulled their community for approximately $1.1 million just hours after a successful mint, abandoning promised game development and a metaverse. In a rare outcome, US federal authorities arrested the perpetrators — making this one of the first NFT rug pulls to result in federal charges.

How to Spot a Rug

The HODL Warriors have developed a field manual for identifying potential rug pulls before they occur. Every warrior should know these red flags:

  • Anonymous developers — No doxxed team members with verifiable identities.
  • No smart contract audit — A project that has not undergone independent security auditing is a significant risk.
  • Unlocked or team-controlled liquidity — Liquidity that can be withdrawn by the team at any time is the mechanism of a rug pull.
  • Sudden social silence — When community channels go quiet, the clock is ticking.
  • Unrealistic promises — 1000x guarantees, revolutionary technology with no technical documentation, and celebrity endorsements without disclosure are classic signals.
  • Sell restrictions — Contracts that prevent or heavily tax selling trap investors for the pull.

The Anti-Rug Covenant

In response to the Rug Pull Wars, the HODL Warriors established the Anti-Rug Covenant — a pledge to practice rigorous DYOR (Do Your Own Research) before entering any project. The Covenant's principles: verify the team, audit the contract, check the liquidity lock, trust the chain data over the Discord hype.

The Covenant is inscribed as a supplement to The Satoshi Scroll — a war-forged addendum that acknowledges the reality of adversaries within the ecosystem itself.
